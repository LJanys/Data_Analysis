1.1 Objective of the course

Review basic methods and models from econometrics and implement them in R. Most of them without using pre-specified functions. Why? Aids in understanding basic methods: building blocks for being able to implement more complicated methods by yourself. Not just user, but developer!

Goal: Being able to evaluate different methods based on their finite sample properties

1.2 List of Topics

  • Nonparametric Density Estimation
  • Nonparametric Regression

An Example: Density estimation

Suppose that \(X \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)\). Compare a randomly chosen sample from a normal distribution with the theoretical values of the normal distribution.

N=100# Number of observations in the draw
x<-rnorm(N, mean=0, sd=1)
f<-rgamma(N,1,1)
z=seq(min(x),max(x),le=100)
hx<-dnorm(z)
plot(density(x),main="",ylim=c(0,0.6)) 
lines(z,hx, col="red")
lines(density(f),col="blue")
